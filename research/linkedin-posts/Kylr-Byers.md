## Post 2
**Date:** 9 months ago
**Topic:** Most brands are accidentally building 
half an AI search strategy

**Content Summary:**
Semrush analysed how AI platforms responded 
to prompts across five major industries:
finance, digital technology, business services, 
consumer electronics, and fashion.

Key finding:
"The brands most frequently mentioned in AI answers 
aren't always the ones most frequently cited 
as authoritative sources."

There is a disconnect between:
- Being MENTIONED in AI answers
- Being cited as an AUTHORITATIVE SOURCE

Most companies are building only one side 
of this equation — accidentally building 
half an AI search strategy without realising it.

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
This finding directly reinforces Lily Ray's 
citation vs recommendation gap and Kevin Indig's 
off-property authority data. 

A complete AI content strategy requires two tracks:
1. Content that gets your brand MENTIONED 
   — topical authority, BOFU pages, comparisons
2. Content that earns AUTHORITATIVE CITATIONS 
   — original research, third-party validation, 
   expert-backed content

Producing content for only one track 
leaves half the AI search opportunity on the table.
