## Post 1
**Date:** 2 days ago
**Topic:** Off-property reputation drives AI citations 
more than on-page optimisation

**Content Summary:**
Two brands can run identical on-page optimisation and get 
cited by AI at completely different rates. The gap is 
off-property reputation.

Key arguments:
- AI reuses trust already attached to sources it pulls from
- AI favours entities it already recognises on a topic
- Your owned blog is one of the WEAKER inputs for AI citations
- Publications, analysts, and competitors that mention you 
  give AI something it can trust without asking you
- On-page work is just one lever — not the primary driver

Data from Growth Memo AEO Visibility Matrix:
- Company topics: 63.6% of AI citations come from 
  THIRD-PARTY sources
- Sell topics: 54.3% from third-party sources
- Research topics: 85% from OWNED sources
- Partner topics: 76.9% from owned sources

Practical recommendation:
"Before you tune another title tag, check which sources 
AI cites in your space and whether any of them mention you."

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
This fundamentally changes the content production strategy. 
Most AI SEO content advice focuses on what you publish 
on your own site. Kevin's data shows that for company and 
commercial topics — exactly the topics B2B SaaS companies 
care about most — the majority of AI citations come from 
third-party sources. This means digital PR, analyst mentions, 
and competitor comparisons matter more than blog posts 
for AI citation visibility.

## Post 2
**Date:** 2 days ago
**Topic:** AI citation sources are topic-specific — 
not universal

**Content Summary:**
AI trusts a different set of sources for every topic. 
There is no fixed list it pulls from.

Data from Growth Memo research across topics:
- Invoicing questions: competitor domains hold 33.5% 
  of AI citations
- Starting a business: same source type holds only 7%
- Financial management: publishers hold 16.4%, 
  competitors 11.2%

Key implications:
- Building mentions everywhere wastes budget
- You cannot copy a PR plan from a neighbouring topic
- You must map the specific domains AI already cites 
  for YOUR specific topic
- Then earn your way into that specific citation set

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
This is one of the most actionable insights in this 
entire research project. Topic-specific citation mapping 
should be the first step before any AI content production 
begins. For a B2B SaaS company — identify what AI cites 
when someone asks questions in your specific category, 
then build content and PR strategy to appear in those 
exact sources.

## Key Insight from Kevin Indig
The central thesis across Kevin's posts is a direct 
challenge to conventional AI SEO content advice:

Publishing more content on your owned site is the 
WEAKEST lever for AI citation visibility on 
commercial and company topics.

The stronger levers are:
1. Third-party mentions from publications AI already trusts
2. Competitor and analyst coverage in your specific topic
3. Topic-specific citation mapping — 
   not generic link building

For B2B SaaS AI content production this means: 
produce content that earns third-party citations, 
not just content that sits on your blog. 
The AI content strategy is really a digital PR 
and authority strategy first, 
content production strategy second.
