## Post 2
**Date:** 1 day ago
**Topic:** Do AI Assistants Actually Render JavaScript 
When Grounding?

**Content Summary:**
Research by Andre Alpar tested whether major AI assistants 
render JavaScript when crawling websites for grounding:

Key findings:
- US AI assistants (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, 
  Perplexity, Meta AI) read RAW HTML ONLY
- Chinese assistants (DeepSeek, ERNIE, Qwen, Kimi) 
  and Mistral DO render JavaScript
- Perplexity told users it could not access a page 
  while its crawler had already fetched it — 
  self-reports are unreliable
- Grok ran JavaScript but still answered from raw HTML
- Server logs are the only reliable source of truth 
  for what AI actually did with your page

Critical implication:
"If your important content is injected by JavaScript — 
client-side frameworks, lazy-loaded sections, content 
behind tabs — then for ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, 
Perplexity and Copilot, that content effectively 
does not exist."

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
This is one of the most important technical findings 
for anyone building AI-visible content. 
If your B2B SaaS website renders content via JavaScript 
frameworks (React, Vue, Next.js client-side), 
AI crawlers cannot read it. 
All your AI-powered SEO content efforts are invisible 
to the platforms you are trying to rank on.
Practical implication: 
Server-side rendering (SSR) is non-negotiable 
for AI search visibility. 
Content must be in the raw HTML — not injected 
after page load.
