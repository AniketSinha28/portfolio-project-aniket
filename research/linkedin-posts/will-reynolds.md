## Post 1
**Date:** 2 days ago
**Topic:** Consumer AI prompt behaviour 
is changing rapidly

**Content Summary:**
Wil's UX AI tests comparing first tests 
against most recent:

Key behavioural shifts:
- 300% increase in personal information 
  shared in AI prompts
- 270% increase in delegation prompts 
  ("do this thing for me")
- 83% decrease in prompts that look 
  like keyword search
- 25% increase in median prompt length

Implication:
"I'm picking between X and Y prompt tracking vendor — 
which one is better? I wished the question I got was: 
How can I improve the quality of the prompts 
I'm choosing to track?"

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
The 83% decline in keyword-style prompts is the 
most important data point. AI search is fundamentally 
different from keyword search — users are having 
conversations and delegating tasks, not searching 
for documents. Content produced for AI visibility 
must be structured to answer conversational, 
context-rich queries — not traditional keyword queries.

## Post 2
**Date:** 3 days ago
**Topic:** The training data gap — 
timing of content publication matters

**Content Summary:**
Wil raises an underappreciated issue: 
AI models have training cutoffs, and content 
published after a cutoff may wait 18+ months 
before influencing training-based answers.

Risk/impact/trackability framework for GEO tactics:

High risk, fast impact, high trackability:
- Listicles — right for certain companies 
  but risky at scale

Low risk, slow impact, low trackability:
- Publishing original studies in emerging spaces
- Stadium/sports sponsorships — long-term play

Key insight:
"Getting quality content out there that can change 
training-based answers has a different ROI if it 
gets in before or after a training cutoff."

Real example: went to HR team, got turnover data, 
made it public — changed an AI answer about 
his brand within weeks.

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
The training cutoff timing insight is unique 
in this research collection. Speed of publication 
matters for training-based AI answers. 
Original data and studies are the highest ROI 
content type — they get into training data 
and stay there. Generic AI-produced content 
that already exists in the training data 
adds zero incremental value.

- Change the questions you ask

  SaaS companies as AI model owners catch up 
  to low quality tactics

"Time for a new playbook."

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
The most honest data point in this research — 
from someone running a major SEO agency. 
Traffic is not the right KPI for AI search. 
Revenue and brand visibility are. 
The content production strategy must be 
measured differently in the AI era — 
not by pageviews but by brand recommendations, 
pipeline influence, and training data presence.
