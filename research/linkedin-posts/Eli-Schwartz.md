## Post 1
**Date:** 21 hours ago
**Topic:** Your homepage should be the AI answer — 
not a comparison page

**Content Summary:**
Leadership is panicking about LLM visibility. 
Eli argues the panic misses the point entirely.

Key argument:
"If your brand has done its job in the market, 
your homepage should be the answer when someone 
asks an LLM for the best option in your category. 
Not a comparison page buried six clicks deep 
or a blog post. The homepage."

Real example — Indeed:
- Indeed's homepage is the main driver 
  of their AI visibility
- Not a help article or long-tail landing page
- "Indeed's SEO team isn't winning that slot 
  through some clever AEO tactic. 
  They're cashing a check that brand marketing 
  wrote years ago."

AEO vs SEO philosophy:
- Traditional SEO goal: breadth — 
  rank for as many queries as possible
- AEO goal: depth — be THE answer 
  for your category
- AEO is closer to a BRANDING channel 
  than a traffic channel

Practical advice:
"Before anyone on your team budgets for a new tool 
or starts a new AEO initiative — stop and look 
at what you've already got. Where does your brand 
show up right now, and is it the page you'd actually 
want representing you?"

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
For B2B SaaS companies this reframes the entire 
content production strategy. The goal is not to 
produce more content pages — it is to build 
brand authority so strong that the homepage 
becomes the default AI answer for the category. 
Content production should serve brand building, 
not replace it.

## Post 2
**Date:** 1 day ago
**Topic:** Google's three pillars for AI search — 
be unique, be helpful, be agent-ready

**Content Summary:**
Google stated that winning in AI search comes 
down to three things:
1. Be unique
2. Be helpful
3. Be agent-ready

No secret schema, no chunking trick, 
no file you upload to make LLMs love your site.

Eli's critique of the AEO tool industry:
"Almost every tactic built around gaming AI answers 
has a shelf life measured in months rather than years, 
because the moment enough people exploit it, 
Google quietly patches it out."

Specific tactics with expiration dates:
- Structured data formatting tricks
- Forum mention farming
- Answer-box keyword stuffing

On AI visibility tracking tools:
"They'll tell you exactly where you rank. 
They will not tell you why, and they definitely 
won't tell you what to do about it, because the 
honest answer is 'write something only you could 
have written,' and that doesn't fit in a 
dashboard widget."

The actual fix:
- Unique: cannot be automated away at scale
- Helpful: cannot be faked long-term
- "Go back to the unglamorous work that never 
  had a clever name: user empathy and marketing"

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
This is the philosophical foundation for the 
entire research project. AI-powered SEO content 
production at scale is fundamentally flawed 
if it produces generic, replicable content. 
The only durable AI content strategy is 
producing content only your brand could produce — 
from proprietary data, genuine customer insight, 
and authentic expertise. Volume without uniqueness 
is a liability, not an asset.
