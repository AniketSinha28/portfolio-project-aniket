## Post 1
**Date:** 2 days ago
**Topic:** B2C vs B2B content — completely different 
LLM traffic story

**Content Summary:**
Analysis of 61 brands' content-type traffic from LLMs:

Key data:
- B2C brands: editorial/blog sections = only 7% of LLM clicks
- B2B brands: editorial/blog sections = more than 25% of clicks

Why the difference:
- B2B has BOFU content — alternative pages, 
  "Best X" pages — that get cited more in LLMs
- B2C equivalent coverage comes from affiliate sites 
  and high-authority publishers — not brand content
- B2B conversion-focused pages get cited more 
  and drive clicks as a byproduct

Strategy implication:
- B2C: focus on affiliate sites, product pages, 
  increasing site authority and mentions in affiliate listings
- B2B: editorial and BOFU content directly 
  drives LLM influence and clicks

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
Directly answers the B2B SaaS content question. 
Editorial and BOFU content (alternatives, comparisons, 
best-of pages) is a legitimate AI traffic driver for B2B — 
unlike B2C where third-party coverage dominates. 
However this must be read alongside Lily Ray's finding 
that self-promotional listicles hand wins to competitors. 
Quality and authenticity of BOFU content matters — 
not volume.

## Post 2
**Date:** 1 week ago
**Topic:** AI traffic converts 34% better than 
organic on average

**Content Summary:**
Data from 77 sites with statistically significant 
AI search traffic:

Key findings:
- AI traffic converts 34% better than organic on average
- B2B: 49% better conversion than organic
- B2C: 19% better conversion than organic
- Early claims of 4x-7x better conversion 
  were outliers — the majority sits close to organic

Why B2B converts better from AI:
- Bottom-funnel transactional content — 
  X vs Y, Y alternatives, best X software
- These comparison types are more prevalent in B2B
- If you lack this content you are missing 
  the AI conversion opportunity

Warning:
"Approximately one in four GA profiles we looked at 
didn't have goals set up at all — 
if you can't figure out these numbers, 
that's a bigger problem you need to fix now."

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
Provides the business case for B2B SaaS AI content 
investment. AI traffic not only exists — it converts 
49% better than organic for B2B. 
Combined with the content types that drive it 
(BOFU, alternatives, comparisons) this is 
a clear ROI argument for investing in 
quality AI-optimised content production.

## Post 3
**Date:** 2 weeks ago
**Topic:** TOFU content is back — AI recommends 
brands on product-connected topics

**Content Summary:**
Hudgens tested product-led top-of-funnel topics 
in AI results — found that company recommendations 
DO appear for brands that have created supporting 
top-funnel content.

Example: HubSpot for "what is CRM" query 
achieved 83% brand visibility in LLM mentions.

Stress test framework:
"If it is possible to achieve at least 50% brand 
visibility on these prompts, it is a top funnel 
topic worth going after if the TOFU has significant 
search volume as a proxy of demand."

Implication:
AI is not killing top-of-funnel content — 
it is rewarding brands that have built 
genuine topical authority through 
product-connected content creation.

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
Reframes the full-funnel AI content strategy. 
Both TOFU (topical authority, product-connected) 
and BOFU (alternatives, comparisons, best-of) 
content have roles in AI visibility — 
but only when connected to genuine brand authority. 
The 50% brand visibility benchmark is a 
practical framework for prioritising 
which topics to produce content for.
