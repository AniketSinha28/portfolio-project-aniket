## Post 1
**Date:** 1 day ago
**Topic:** Self-promotional listicles are handing 
wins to your competitors in AI Overviews

**Content Summary:**
Lily pulled 100 B2B "best [category]" queries from 
Google AI Overviews across three dates April–June 2026.

Key finding — citation vs recommendation are 
completely different outcomes:
- When a brand's own self-promotional listicle 
  got cited, that brand was LEFT OUT of the actual 
  recommendation 69% of the time
- The recommendations went to the established 
  competitors listed IN their own article
- "You write a listicle, name your competitors, 
  call yourself the best, and Google cites you 
  as the source while recommending everyone else"

Google has decoupled what it cites from who it recommends:
- Citations: anchored to who published the content
- Recommendations: anchored to actual authority 
  (who the rest of the web talks about and links to)

Additional signals:
- Google demoted many of these sites in organic search 
  in January — drops worsened during May core update
- AI Overviews adding disclaimers warning categories 
  are "saturated with self-proclaimed experts"
- Claude does the same thing

"If AI citations are your main KPI, I'd reconsider. 
Being recommended is much more valuable."

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
This is the most important finding for B2B SaaS 
AI content strategy. The popular advice to 
"publish best-of listicles to appear in AI answers" 
is actively counterproductive for brands without 
existing third-party authority. The content strategy 
must focus on RECOMMENDATIONS not just CITATIONS — 
which requires building genuine off-property authority, 
not self-promotional content production at scale.

## Post 3
**Date:** 3 days ago
**Topic:** Comparison and alternative pages — 
the next spam frontier

**Content Summary:**
Brand vs. competitor comparison pages 
("Brand X vs Y", "Competitor Alternatives") 
have been widely recommended as an SEO/GEO tactic. 
Lily argues this is the next wave of content 
Google and AI companies will crack down on.

Key arguments:
- Once a tactic works it gets scaled — 
  and scaling kills it
- AI content generation makes it easy to 
  spin up hundreds of comparison pages — 
  this is exactly what is getting sites penalised
- Google's product review criteria requires 
  PROOF you actually tested the product
- For comparison pages this would mean 
  actually hiring and testing competitors — 
  almost no brand does this
- What happens instead: brands use negative 
  competitor reviews or fabricate information
- Google, OpenAI and AI companies are 
  increasingly leaning on OBJECTIVE third-party 
  reviews — Reddit, YouTube, Trustpilot, G2 — 
  instead of biased brand content

"Building these pages at scale — and doing it 
inauthentically — is risky. When too many sites 
adopt these approaches, they become a liability."

**Why this matters for AI-powered SEO content production:**
A direct warning against one of the most popular 
AI content production tactics for B2B SaaS — 
mass-produced comparison pages. The AI content 
production strategy cannot be built around 
self-promotional content at scale. 
It must be built around genuine, verifiable, 
experience-backed content — or earning mentions 
in the third-party sources AI actually trusts 
(Reddit, G2, Trustpilot, YouTube).

## Key Insight from Lily Ray
Two findings from Lily Ray fundamentally 
challenge the mainstream AI SEO content 
production playbook:

1. Citation ≠ Recommendation
   Being cited by AI and being recommended by AI 
   are completely different outcomes. 
   Self-promotional content gets you cited 
   but gets your competitors recommended. 
   The KPI should be recommendations, not citations.

2. Authentic experience is the only durable signal
   AI companies are actively working to filter out 
   biased brand content and lean on objective 
   third-party sources. 
   Any AI content production strategy built on 
   self-promotion at scale is building on sand.

Combined with Kevin Indig's citation data, 
the picture is clear: 
AI-powered SEO content production for B2B SaaS 
must be anchored in genuine expertise, 
third-party validation, and authentic experience — 
not volume and self-promotion.
